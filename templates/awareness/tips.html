Essential Phishing Protection Tips

Learn how to identify and protect yourself from phishing attacks

Suspicious sender addresses

Urgent or threatening language

"Your account will be suspended in 24 hours!" or "Immediate action required!"

Generic greetings

"Dear Customer" instead of your actual name when the company should know you.

Poor spelling and grammar

Legitimate companies usually have professional communication standards.

üîó Link Safety

Hover before clicking

Hover over links to see the actual destination URL before clicking.

Check for URL shorteners

Be cautious of bit.ly, tinyurl.com, or other shortened URLs that hide the real destination.

Look for HTTPS

Legitimate sites handling sensitive data should use HTTPS (look for the lock icon).

Verify domain names

PayPal.com vs PayPaI.com (with an 'I' instead of 'l')

Use two-factor authentication (2FA)

Even if your password is stolen, 2FA provides an additional security layer.

Keep software updated

Updates often include security patches that protect against new threats.

Use a password manager

Password managers won't auto-fill credentials on fake websites.

Verify independently

When in doubt, contact the organization directly through their official website or phone number.
